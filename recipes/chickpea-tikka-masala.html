Chickpea Tikka Masala

Description

Chickpea tikka masala is a delightful and hearty vegetarian dish that's become a beloved favorite in both Indian cuisine and international dining. At the heart of this flavorful creation are tender chickpeas, which soak up the rich and aromatic tomato-based gravy infused with a medley of spices. The chickpeas are often marinated in a yogurt and spice blend, allowing them to absorb the vibrant flavors before being simmered to perfection. This dish is characterized by its bold and complex taste profile, with a harmonious blend of spices such as cumin, coriander, garam masala, and paprika, lending layers of warmth and depth to every bite. The addition of cream or coconut milk brings a creamy, indulgent texture to the dish, perfectly balancing the spices and creating a luxurious mouthfeel.

Chickpea tikka masala is not only a flavorful culinary masterpiece but also a versatile and nutritious choice. As a vegetarian option, it provides a protein-packed alternative to meat-based curries, making it a go-to for those seeking a plant-based meal that doesn't compromise on taste or satisfaction. Whether served over a bed of fragrant basmati rice or accompanied by warm naan bread, chickpea tikka masala is a comfort food that transcends borders, bringing people together over its inviting aroma and mouthwatering flavors. Its popularity has grown globally, highlighting the universal appeal of this Indian-inspired dish that offers a hearty, satisfying, and wholesome dining experience for all.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 carrots, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/8 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ginger paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1/2 medium fresh jalapeño chile pepper, finely chopped*
  • 2 (15-ounce) cans chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 3/4 cup water, or as needed
  • 1/4 cup half and half
  • 1/4 cup snipped fresh cilantro
  • 4 cups hot cooked basmati rice

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.

  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high. Add onion and carrots and cook until tender, 5 to 6 minutes.

  3. Stir in garam masala, cumin, and black pepper; cook and stir until mixture is fragrant, about 30 seconds.

  4. Add tomato paste, ginger paste, garlic paste, and jalapeño pepper; cook and stir for 2 minutes.

  5. Stir in chickpeas, tomato sauce, sugar, salt, and cayenne pepper. Cook over medium-high heat until mixture is hot. Reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 10 minutes.

  6. Stir in 3/4 cup water and half-and-half. Continue cooking on low until heated through, about 1 minute. For a saucier mixture, add more water if desired. Remove from heat; cover and keep warm.

  7. Top with cilantro and serve with hot cooked basmati rice and/or naan.
